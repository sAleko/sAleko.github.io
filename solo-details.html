Solo Flying - Game/Sim Demo

I made this project for the Congressional App Challenge (CAC) of 2020 for middle and high schoolers. My goal in the CAC was to create and submit an original app for a chance to win in my congressional district. My high school encouraged our class to compete in it, and I also did the project in 2018 with a team, and we won our district that year, but in 2020 I completed it solo. In this project, I learned how to make an arcade-style flight simulator from start to finish, from what was needed to make a menu, to creating an environment, to creating a flying plane you control to explore and compete in a mini-game.

I was inspired to make this because I've used many games and simulators and was really interested and excited by them, so I wanted to try and make my own. The CAC deadline was a good goal to keep me motivated to make this a complete experience. I learned how to implement player movement in 3 dimensions, how to make a menu and a pause menu, how to create an environment, how to make a mini-game, and how to make it all interactable with Virtual Reality controllers. I also learned how to manage my project and time, being a solo team for this challenge.

Challenge

One of the biggest challenges I faced while developing my project for the Congressional App Challenge (CAC) 2020 was creating a terrain for my arcade-style flight simulator. Designing a semi-realistic and engaging environment was essential for the game, but manually crafting a large, interesting world proved to be difficult and time-consuming.

Solution

To overcome this, I utilized a procedural terrain generator, which allowed me to dynamically create landscapes without having to model them by hand. This not only saved time but also ensured that the terrain remained varied and engaging.
